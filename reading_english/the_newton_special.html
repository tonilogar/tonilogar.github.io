The Gravity of Curiosity

Level: B2 | The Complete Chronos Saga of Isaac Newton

1. Match the Words (Pre-reading)

Click an English word, then click its Spanish translation. Theme: Science, Rivalry, and Fate.

💡 Grammar Tip: This story uses a mix of B2 Grammar (Conditionals, Passive Voice, Relative Clauses). Enjoy this extended journey through the life of a genius who wasn't a superhero, just a very curious man.

Chapter I: The Rejected Child

On Christmas Day in 1642, a baby was born in a small stone house in Woolsthorpe, England. The baby was premature and incredibly weak. The Order, a secret organization that controlled time, had manipulated the probabilities to ensure this child would not survive. They knew his future, and they feared his mind.

Jack stood outside the house, smoking a cigar in the freezing snow. "Silas," Jack whispered into his communicator. "The baby is dying. The Order's manipulation is working. We need a medical intervention, but I am just a detective."

"I know, Jack," Silas replied. "That is why I recruited someone new. Look behind you."

A quantum portal opened silently in the snow. A woman stepped out, carrying a futuristic medical bag. "My name is Dr. Evelyn Vance," she said quickly. "I am a biological engineer from the 22nd century. Silas recruited me ten minutes ago." She rushed into the house, completely invisible to the mother, and used advanced nanotech to strengthen the baby's lungs. The child survived. His name was Isaac Newton.

But The Order didn't give up. When Isaac was three years old, his father died, and his mother abandoned him to marry a rich man. Isaac was raised by his grandparents, feeling alone and rejected. Jack and Evelyn watched him grow, protecting him from the shadows as the sad, lonely boy spent his days building mechanical toys instead of playing with other children.

Chapter II: The Sizar of Cambridge

Years later, Isaac managed to get into Cambridge University. However, because he was poor, he had to work as a "sizar"—a servant who cleaned the rooms and served food to the rich students. The Order saw an opportunity. They infiltrated the university and encouraged the wealthy students to bully Isaac, hoping to break his spirit and force him to quit science.

Jack sat on the roof of Trinity College, watching Isaac clean boots in the rain. "They are crushing him, Evelyn," Jack said. "He is depressed. He is going to give up."

"We cannot interfere directly," Evelyn said, adjusting her glasses. "But we can level the playing field." Evelyn secretly replaced the books of the bullies with advanced mathematical puzzles that confused them, while leaving rare astronomy books in Isaac's path. Isaac ignored the bullies and buried himself in the books. His desire to learn was stronger than the humiliation.

Chapter III: The Biological Weapon

By 1665, The Order was desperate. Isaac was too resilient. They decided to take extreme measures. "If we cannot stop the student," the leader of The Order said, "we will shut down the university."

Agents of The Order traveled to London and released a devastating biological weapon: the bacterium Yersinia pestis. The Great Plague of London began. Thousands died, and a terrifying panic spread across England. As The Order expected, Cambridge University was forced to close its doors, and all students were sent home.

Jack watched the carts of dead bodies in London. "Silas, they have gone too far," Jack yelled into his radio. "They started a plague just to stop one student!"

Chapter IV: The Backfire

Isaac Newton returned to his small family farm in Woolsthorpe to escape the plague. The Order celebrated their victory. They assumed that sitting on a boring farm, surrounded by sheep and apples, would destroy Newton's scientific career.

But the plan backfired completely. In fact, it "salió rana" (it went completely wrong for them). Without the distractions of the university, without the bullying, and without the strict curriculum, Newton's mind was finally free. The absolute isolation gave him the perfect environment to think.

Silas laughed through the communicator. "Jack, The Order just made their biggest mistake. They thought the plague would stop him. Instead, the plague just became a natural part of the timeline, giving him the exact isolation he needed. It's the Annus Mirabilis—the Year of Wonders."

Chapter V: The Prism and the Apple

The Order realized their mistake and sent physical assassins to the farm. But Jack was ready. He had called for backup. Tenzin, the time-traveling monk, meditated at the front gate, while Jack and Evelyn guarded the garden. When the assassins arrived, Jack's EMP weapons and Tenzin's martial arts neutralized them silently.

Inside the house, completely unaware of the temporal war outside, a bored Newton was playing with a glass prism in a dark room, discovering that white light was made of a rainbow of colors.

Later that afternoon, Newton sat in the garden. Tenzin watched him from the branches of a tree. A gentle wind blew, and an apple fell to the ground. Newton stared at it. If gravity can pull the apple, he thought, can it reach the moon? The universe had just been decoded.

Chapter VI: The Giants

Decades passed. Newton was now older, a professor, but the war wasn't over. The Order had found a new strategy: Robert Hooke. Hooke was a brilliant scientist, but The Order manipulated his ego, making him aggressively attack Newton in public and claim that he had discovered gravity first.

The constant rivalry and bullying from Hooke made Newton paranoid and reclusive. He locked himself in his room and refused to publish his greatest work. In a letter to Hooke, Newton wrote a famous, passive-aggressive sentence: "If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants." He meant that he learned from the great scientists of the past, but it was also a cruel joke, because Hooke was a very short man.

Chapter VII: The Final Push

It was 1684. Newton's masterpiece, a book containing the laws of physics and calculus, was sitting on his desk, covered in dust. He refused to publish it because he hated the scientific community.

"If he doesn't publish the Principia Mathematica, the timeline collapses," Jack told Silas. "Who can we send?"

"I have recruited a historical ally," Silas replied. "A young, rich astronomer named Edmond Halley. He doesn't know about time travel, but I gave him a suggestion in his dreams."

Halley visited Newton at Cambridge. He was amazed by Newton's mathematics. When Newton said he couldn't afford to print the book, Halley smiled. "I will pay for everything," Halley promised.

Jack, Evelyn, and Tenzin stood on a rooftop in London in 1687, watching the first copies of the Principia Mathematica being sold. The Order was permanently defeated in this era. The laws of the universe were finally written on paper.

"He wasn't a superhero," Evelyn smiled. "He was paranoid, grumpy, and difficult."

"Yeah," Jack said, lighting a cigar. "But he was curious. And sometimes, that's all it takes to change the world."

[End of The Complete Newton Saga]
