The Lost Boarding Pass

Level: B1 | Grammar: Past Perfect | Topic: The Airport

1. Before You Read: Vocabulary Match

Match the English words with their Spanish translation before starting the story.

2. Read the Story

💡 Tip: Double-click or select any word you don't know to translate and save it to My Vocabulary!

Sarah arrived at London Heathrow Airport at 5:00 AM. She had prepared for this important business trip to Tokyo for months. She had packed her luggage three days in advance, and she had printed all her documents the night before. Everything seemed perfect. She walked confidently toward the check-in desk, relieved that she had not forgotten her passport.

After she had checked in her bags, Sarah decided to get some coffee. She found a small café near the security checkpoint. She bought a cappuccino and a croissant. She sat at a corner table and reviewed her presentation on her laptop. By the time she had finished her breakfast, the airport had become incredibly busy. The quiet morning atmosphere had completely vanished.

She checked the departure boards. Her flight to Tokyo was scheduled for Gate 42. Sarah packed her laptop into her bag and walked toward the security checkpoint. As she reached the front of the line, the security officer asked for her documents. Sarah reached into her coat pocket, but it was empty. She checked her handbag. Nothing. Panic set in. She realized she had lost her boarding pass!

She stepped out of the line and tried to calm down. Where had she left it? She thought about every place she had been since she had arrived at the airport. She knew she had held the boarding pass after she left the check-in desk. Then, she had gone to the café. She ran back to the café as fast as she could.

When she arrived, a new customer had already occupied her table. She asked the waitress if anyone had found a boarding pass. The waitress smiled warmly and reached under the counter. "Yes, a flight attendant had found it on the floor ten minutes ago and gave it to me," she said. Sarah sighed with relief. She had never felt so happy in her entire life. She thanked the waitress, rushed through the security checkpoint, and managed to board her flight just in time for take-off.
