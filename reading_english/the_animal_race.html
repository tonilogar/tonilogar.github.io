The Animal Race

Level: A2 (Elementary) | Focus: Comparatives & Superlatives

1. Match the Words (Pre-reading)

Click an English word, then click its Spanish translation.

Once upon a time, in the middle of a vast jungle, the animals argued about who was the best. The lion, the king of the jungle, decided to organize a competition. "We will have a great race," the lion roared. "The winner will be the greatest animal of all." Every animal wanted to participate.

The cheetah was very confident. "I am faster than everyone," he said. "I am the fastest animal in the world. This race will be easier than catching a mouse." The turtle heard him and smiled slowly. "You are faster than me, but I am more patient than you," she said.

The elephant was the largest competitor. "I am heavier than the cheetah, and I am stronger than the tiger," he proclaimed. "I am the strongest animal in the forest. Nothing can stop me." The monkey laughed from a tree. "But you are slower than me! I can climb higher than anyone. I am the most agile!"

The race began the next morning. The path went through the jungle, across a deep river, and up a high mountain. The cheetah started first. He ran faster than the wind. He was the quickest runner, but when he reached the river, he stopped. The river was too deep, and he was a terrible swimmer.

The elephant arrived at the river. He was heavier than the water, so he walked straight through it. He was the most powerful animal, but when he reached the mountain, he could not climb the rocks. The monkey climbed the mountain easily because he was lighter than the elephant, but he got lost in the forest.

In the end, no single animal could finish the race alone. The eagle, who flew higher than all of them, watched from the sky. "Do you see?" the eagle called out. "The cheetah is the fastest, the elephant is the strongest, and the monkey is the most agile. Every animal is better than the others at something. We are all the best in our own way."

2. Comprehension Quiz (Cuestionario)

1. Who thought the race would be easier than catching a mouse?

2. What did the elephant say about himself?

3. Why did the cheetah stop at the river?

4. What was the lesson of the story?
