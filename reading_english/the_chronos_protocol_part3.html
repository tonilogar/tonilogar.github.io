The Siege of Chronos

Jack opened his eyes quickly. He was not in his dark city. He was not in his old bar. He was back in the Chronos Agency. The beautiful stars were still shining outside the window, but the agency was completely different. The lights were flashing red dangerously. A loud alarm was ringing.

He remembered everything. He didn't forget the Titanic. He remembered Mr. Sterling and the little girl.

"Why do I remember?" Jack asked loudly.

"Because the timeline is broken," a woman's voice answered. Jack turned around. A tall woman in a futuristic silver uniform was standing near him. She was holding a large weapon tightly. "I am Captain Elara. I come from the year 2150. If we don't hurry, we will die here."

Jack looked at her. "Where is The Monk?"

"He is in the Hall of Fallen Legends," Elara said sadly. "The Order tracked your quantum signal from 1912. They are attacking the agency right now. They will destroy everything if we don't protect him."

Jack picked up a weapon from the floor. He felt adrenaline in his blood. "I will help you. If they break the main door, we will fight them together."

They ran fast to the Hall of Fallen Legends. The Monk was sitting on the floor in the center of the statues. He was bleeding, but his face was deeply compassionate. He was meditating quietly.

"Monk! We will take you to a safe time!" Jack shouted. "We won't let them kill you."

The Monk opened his eyes slowly. "You remembered the mission, Jack. That means you are not a normal recruit anymore. You are a true Keeper now. But I will not stay in this room. If I stay here, they will destroy the timeline."

Suddenly, the massive metal doors exploded loudly. Ten soldiers from The Order walked in. They were wearing black armor. Their leader pointed a glowing gun at The Monk.

"The time of anarchists is over," the leader said coldly. "We will rule the past and the future."

Captain Elara looked at Jack. "If we shoot now, we will have a chance," she whispered.

Before they could shoot, The Monk stood up. He raised his hands calmly, not to attack, but to protect. The air in the room stopped moving. The soldiers from The Order froze completely. The bullets stopped in the air. Time stopped.

The Monk looked at Jack and Elara. "A Buddhist monk must practice compassion. Leaving you to fight alone is not compassion. I am tired of watching from the center of time. I will not be just a guide anymore."

"What will you do?" Jack asked.

"I will join you," The Monk smiled peacefully. He closed his hands, and eight of the frozen soldiers disappeared into a portal. But two soldiers suddenly unfroze and attacked him.

The Monk didn't lose his smile. Moving with perfect calmness, he used beautiful martial arts. In two fluid movements, he neutralized both soldiers. They fell to the floor, asleep.

The Monk fixed his clothes slowly. "It is a dirty job," he said without altering his voice, "but someone has to do it. I will travel with you. The three of us will protect the universe together."

Jack looked at the empty room. He looked at Elara, and he looked at The Monk. His old life was boring and grey. This was where he belonged.

"I won't go back to the city," Jack said firmly. "I will stay with you."

Captain Elara touched Jack's shoulder and smiled at The Monk. "Welcome to the team. Tomorrow, we will have a lot of work."
