The First Day of Class

Level: B1 (Intermediate) | Focus: Consistency & Spiral Learning

1. Match the Words (Pre-reading)

Click an English word, then click its Spanish translation.

It was exactly 9:00 AM on a Monday morning. The new English students sat in their chairs in the classroom. They looked nervously at the white board on the wall. Everyone was quiet. Suddenly, the door opened and the teacher walked in.

"Good morning, everyone," she said with a bright smile. "Welcome to your English class." She wrote her name on the board. "My name is Sarah. I have taught English for ten years. But I started my career as a student, just like you. I learned languages when I was young, so I know exactly how you feel right now."

A student raised his hand. "Teacher, I am afraid of speaking. I think English is more difficult than my native language." Sarah smiled gently. "Do not worry," she said. "Making mistakes is normal. In fact, making mistakes is better than staying silent. Every mistake is a step forward."

"What are we going to do in this course?" another student asked. "We are going to practice listening and speaking every single day," Sarah explained. "We are going to work hard, but we are also going to have fun."

She walked to the center of the room. "I want to share a secret with you," she whispered. "Many people think you need special talent to be successful. That is not true. The real secret is consistency."

"What does consistency mean?" a student asked. Sarah pointed at the calendar on her desk. "It means studying a little bit every day. Studying fifteen minutes every day is much better than studying three hours on a Sunday. If you want to reach your goal, you must practice daily."

The students smiled and opened their books. They felt more confident now. They knew the journey was going to be long, but with consistency and a good teacher, they were ready to learn.
