The Blackout in the Pyrenees

Level: B1+ (Advanced) | Focus: Past Continuous vs Past Simple (Interrupted Actions)

1. Match the Words (Pre-reading)

Click an English word, then click its Spanish translation.

💡 Tip: This is a longer, more complex story. Pay attention to how the Past Continuous (actions in progress) is interrupted by the Past Simple (sudden events).

Dr. Aris and his team of five scientists are stationed at an isolated meteorological observatory at the very top of the Pyrenees mountains. They have worked here for six months without leaving the building. Their mission is to study extreme weather changes, but they have never seen a storm like the one approaching tonight.

It was midnight. Outside, a terrible blizzard was covering the mountains in thick white snow. The wind was howling like a hungry wolf against the windows, and the temperature was dropping rapidly. Inside, the laboratory felt warm and safe, but everyone was nervous.

Dr. Aris was analyzing the latest atmospheric data on his computer when suddenly, a massive lightning bolt hit the main antenna. Instantly, the lights went out. The screens turned black, and a deep silence filled the room. It was a complete blackout.

"Do not panic!" Aris shouted in the darkness. "The backup system should start automatically." But nothing happened. The building was completely silent. Without electricity, the heating system would fail. "If we don't fix the power soon, we will freeze to death," his colleague Elena warned.

Elena turned on her flashlight. "I am going to check the emergency generator in the basement," she announced bravely. Aris grabbed a heavy coat and another flashlight. "It is too dangerous to go alone. I will go with you," he replied without hesitation.

The two scientists walked down the dark, narrow stairs to the basement. It was much colder than the upper floors. Every shadow looked menacing. While they were walking through the dark corridor, they heard a strange interruption in the silence. It was a loud, metallic sound coming from the main entrance door.

They stopped breathing. Someone, or something, was hitting the heavy metal door from the outside. "This is impossible," Aris whispered, feeling absolutely terrified. "Nobody can survive a blizzard like this. The closest town is fifty kilometers away."

"If we open that door, we will let the freezing storm inside," Elena said, her voice shaking. "But if there is a lost climber out there, they will die in minutes," Aris argued. As a scientist, he needed to investigate the source of the noise.

While Aris was pushing the heavy security bar to unlock the door, the banging suddenly stopped. He pulled the door open, letting the violent wind and snow rush into the building. He pointed his flashlight into the dark storm. There was nobody there.

However, the snow told a different story. In the light of the flashlight, Aris saw massive, non-human footprints in the snow, leading away from the door and disappearing into the dark forest. "What on earth made those?" Elena asked, staring at the huge marks.

They quickly closed and locked the heavy door, grateful for their safety. They ran to the basement, found the broken fuse, and finally repaired the generator. The lights flickered and came back on, bringing warmth back to the observatory.

When they returned to the laboratory, the main computer was printing a new, unknown signal. It was coming from the exact direction where the footprints had disappeared. Dr. Aris looked at Elena. They both knew that this emergency was not just a weather problem. It was going to be a very long and terrifying night.

2. Comprehension Quiz (Cuestionario)

1. What was Dr. Aris doing when the lightning hit the antenna?

2. What happens if they don't fix the power soon?

3. What interrupted them while they were walking to the basement?

4. What did they find when they opened the door?
