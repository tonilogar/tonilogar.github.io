Next Summer

Level: A2 (Elementary) | Focus: Future with 'Going To'

1. Match the Words (Pre-reading)

Click an English word, then click its Spanish translation.

💡 Tip: Double-click or select any word you don't know to translate and save it to My Vocabulary!

The Smith family sat in the living room at 8 o'clock in the evening. They were very excited. They are going to plan their next summer vacation. "Where are we going to go?" asked the youngest son, Leo. "We are going to travel to a sunny coastal city in Italy in July," his mother answered. She showed them a map on the table. "It is going to be a wonderful trip."

"What are we going to do there?" asked his sister, Maya. "Are we going to swim in the sea every day?" Her father smiled. "Yes, but we are also going to do some sightseeing. We are going to visit old castles, and a tour guide is going to show us the city center. It is going to be very interesting."

"What about the weather?" Leo asked. "I watched the weather forecast," his mother said. "It is going to be very hot and sunny. We are not going to need warm clothes." "Great!" said Maya. "I am going to pack my favorite sunglasses and my new swimsuit."

"Are we going to need an umbrella?" asked the father. "No," replied the mother, "the forecast says it is not going to rain. So, we are not going to pack an umbrella. But we are going to need a big suitcase for all our things."

"How are we going to travel to the airport?" Leo asked. "We are going to take a train," his father explained. "The train station is near our house. We are going to buy the ticket on Monday morning, and we will arrive at the airport at noon. After that, we are going to pack our bags and get ready for the flight." Everyone smiled. It was going to be the best summer ever.

2. Comprehension Quiz (Cuestionario)

1. Where is the family going to travel?

2. What are they going to do besides swimming?

3. Why are they NOT going to pack an umbrella?

4. How are they going to travel to the airport?
