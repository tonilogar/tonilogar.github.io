The Lost Key

Level: Beginner

Vocabulary (Vocabulario)

  • Beautiful: Hermoso/a
  • Afternoon: Tarde
  • Pillow: Almohada
  • Downstairs: Abajo (por las escaleras)
  • Make a cake: Hacer un pastel
  • Sofa: Sofá
  • Behind: Detrás
  • Sad: Triste
  • Grass: Césped / Hierba
  • Hug: Abrazo

Tom is a young boy. He lives in a big house with a small, beautiful garden. Every afternoon, after school, he likes to play in his bedroom. One day, Tom wants to open his special box of toys, but he cannot find his key. It is a small, silver key. He looks on his bed, under his pillow, and inside his closet. The key is not there.

Tom walks downstairs. "Where is my key?" he asks his mother. His mother is in the kitchen. She is making a chocolate cake. She stops mixing and looks at him. "Did you look in the living room?" she asks.

Tom goes to the living room. He looks under the sofa and behind the television. He finds an old coin and a blue sock, but no key. He is starting to feel sad. Then, his mother asks, "Did you look in the garden? You played there yesterday."

Tom opens the back door and goes to the garden. The sun is shining, and the birds are singing. He sees his dog, Max. Max is sitting on the grass. Max has something in his mouth. It is shiny and silver. It is the key! Max is a good dog. Tom takes the key, smiles, and gives Max a big hug. Tom is very happy.

Comprehension Quiz (Cuestionario)

1. Where does Tom look for the key first?

2. What is Tom's mother doing in the kitchen?

3. What does Tom find in the living room?

4. Who has the key?
